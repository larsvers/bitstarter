The single biggest problem a company faces prior to product launch: only the market knows how it will perform. As the owner or investor you might have the best self-selling idea with rocking features, but you can only have an educated guess about where it's maximum potential lies. One question surely asked is 'at what price will my product return the highest revenue?' It's a simple ask. But the best answer requires a little work. Work your project is worth and Predikt is built for. Pricing research is considered to be the trickiest of exercises. Simply asking people directly for a fair product-price leads to low answers based on bargaining behavior.

It's like shopping for your target group. Predikt simulates your product in its market and then asks your target group to interact with it. It's like going to the shop and pondering all the options.
In this process, your product will get many guises (or levels as we call it) in key product-dimensions (or attributes) you would like to test. These can be price, features, design, special offers, or whatever you deem test-worthy. Some of the people will prefer your product for price A with features 1,2 and 3 in a flat-design and for the first 3 months for free. Others will prefer higher price C with features 1-5, all green but without any initial offer. Your respondents will give us a preference for each product version we test. Predikt will then add its magic and decompose this preference for each level we'll test.

It's like playing the market for you. All this information will be fed into a simulator - imagine a building-set - enabling you to virtually play around with all tested building blocks in order to find the highest preference for your product or product-tiers. Or your revenue maximum calculated by preference x tested price-points. It will further give you so called price-elasticity showing at which price point people want to buy or at which price they would leave or simply not buy. It will also show which feature is the biggest purchase driver, which design is the most successful and which offer is the most convincing sales argument. Playing doesn't stop at your product. You can simulate your competitors as well. Will they change prices when you enter the market? If so how will that impact your sales? How your attrition? How will they be affected by your entrance or price change? It's like playing checkers, but knowing all the moves
