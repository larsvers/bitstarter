The single biggest problem a company faces prior to product launch: only the market knows how it will perform. As the owner or investor you might have the best self-selling idea with rocking features, but you can only have an educated guess about where it's maximum potential lies. One question surely asked is 'at what price will my product return the highest revenue?' It's a simple ask. But a truthful answer requires a little work. Work your project is worth and Predikt is built for.

Pricing research is considered to be the trickiest of exercises. Simply asking people directly for a price leads to low answers based on bargaining behaviour and neglects the importance of other product features and, well, the market. The purchase decision is a complex animal.

It's like shopping for your target group. Predikt simulates your product in its market and then asks your target group to interact with it. It's like going to the shop, pondering all the options, before picking the chosen one.
As it's so much fun, we'll repeat this a few times. In this process, your product will get many guises (or levels as we call it) in key product-dimensions (or attributes) you would like to test. These can be price, features, design, special offers, or whatever you deem test-worthy. Some of the people will prefer your product for price A with features 1,2 and 3 in a flat-design and for the first 3 months for free. Others would infact pay a higher price C for more features 1-5, all green but without any initial offer. Your respondents will give us a preference for each product version we test. Predikt will then add its magic and decompose this preference for each level we test.

It's like playing the market for you. All this information will be fed into a simulator (imagine a building-set) enabling you to play with the tested attributes in order to find the highest preference for your product or product-tiers. Or your revenue maximum calculated by preference x tested price-points . It gives you so called price-elasticity showing at which price point people want to buy or at which price they would leave or simply not buy. It shows which feature is the biggest purchase driver, which design is the most successful and which offer is the most convincing sales argument. Playing doesn't stop at your product. You can simulate your competitors as well. Will they change prices when you enter the market? If so how will that impact your sales? How your attrition? How will they be affected by your entrance or price change? It's like playing checkers, but knowing all the moves

FAQ

What's the research technique called Predikt applies?
The technique is called Conjoint. It has been developped in the 1970's and enjoys increasing application particularly in the world of marketing as probably the most effective tool to model and predict product launches holistically.
What will Predikt cost?
It will cost significantly less than comparable research designs cost at the moment. There will be a cost connected to the fieldwork (asking the target group) but - again - the cost for using the Predikt tool will be drastically reduced to comparable research designs in the wild.
What can Predikt do that A/B Testing can't?
There are 3 reasons Predikt clearly rules:
(1) It allows for much more fine-tuned results. You can test several levels of various product dimensions at once and see exactly how each dimension (like price vs design) and level (like 1.99 vs 2.99) moves your preference-share.
(2) It can be used before you have any tangible product. An idea of a product is enough to test if and how it will work. This can go straight into your business case. To convince yourself or anyone out there who needs solid convincing.
(3) You can simulate your competition. You can see how the market will be impacted and where your new customers come from.
It's pro-active rather than re-active.
I'm developping a free app and want to finance it through advertising. Can Predikt still help?
Yes (you guessed right). Price is only one attribute and indeed the most important one for many businesses. But you can leave it out entirely and focus Predikt on other attributes like types, placement or amount of advertising, next to any other attributes you'd like to test as exemplified above.
