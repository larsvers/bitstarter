One simple question every new product has is 'Will I work?' One often falls short answering it, as the only creature that really can is the market. To be precise: The only one who really can is the customer in the marketplace.

Probably best would then be to develop, design and build the product (a space-craft in the shape of a telephone booth as an obvious example) add a price to it and ship it. If that doesn't really work we'll try another price. no? 3rd price. lower. better. But now we have to get rid of this one feature (nitro boost). now the price doesn't work anymore. Oomph. Maybe an offer?!

Product and particularly Pricing research is a complex animal Predikt knows well. Let's have a look at what it takes and what it does.

(1)   Input your target group, various price-levels and some product features you imagine
(2)   Input some info on competitors (if there are any)
(3)   Predikt runs off and calculates a test-design covering all these bases
(4)   It runs on to test these concepts in your market
(5)   And comes back with a snappy simulator answering all key questions you might have in a playful way

It's like playing the market for you. Just copy/paste what you've got in your business case already. What's your product, what are the features and - equally important - what iterations of these features are there ($200? test. $300? test. ($375.99? yep. test please.) Who are the competitors and who in their right mind will buy it. See it as a necessary exercise if you haven't worked on the business case details yet.

Then comes Predikt. It'll go and find answers for you the only valid way - it will simulate the purchase situation for your target group (talkative aeronauts for example). We'll find the right people and show them what's available in the market. They will browse through the products with all their features as they do in a shop or in front of their screens and - pick one.

Now, that's not very clever yet. It's a little bit clever but rather just sensible, as research is truest when realest. The clever thing is that Predikt then adds some analysis-magic that will take each product-dimension we tested apart and enables you to see the impact of each feature and price on your product's preference- or market share. In isolation and in combination with any number of levels you tested. Having both, prices and preference, you can simulate your revenue (a) at all prices within the price spectrum you set and (b) for all the tested product features. It's like playing checkers, but knowing all the moves. honestly.

FAQ

What's the technique Predikt applies?
The technique is called Conjoint. It has been developped in the 1970's and enjoys increasing application particularly in the world of marketing as probably the most effective tool to model and predict product launches holistically.
What will Predikt cost?
In short: it'll be much much cheaper using Predikt than comparable research designs in the wild.
Run by a traditional research agency, you'd currently pay a juicy amount of money for comparable research with at least 5 zeros after the first digit (in Dollars/Euros/Pounds that is). Many of the applied proceses can, however, be automated clearly leading to significant cost-reductions (cutting off up to 3 zeros) as well as even design-benefits! Additionally there are more cost-reducers in the pipeline, apart from automation...
What can Predikt do that A/B Testing can't?
There are 3 reasons Predikt clearly rules:
(1) It allows for much more fine-tuned results. You can test several levels of various product dimensions at once and see exactly how each dimension (like price vs design) and level (like 1.99 vs 2.99) moves your preference-share.
(2) It can be used before you have any tangible product. An idea of a product is enough to test if and how it will work. This can go straight into your business case. To convince yourself or anyone out there who needs solid convincing.
(3) You can simulate your competition. You can see how the market will be impacted and where your new customers come from.
It's pro-active rather than re-active.
I'm developping a free app and want to finance it through advertising. Can Predikt still help?
Yes (you guessed right). Price is only one attribute and indeed the most important one for many businesses. But you can leave it out entirely and focus Predikt on other attributes like types, placement or amount of advertising, next to any other attributes you'd like to test as exemplified above.
